About W3C

The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) is an international community where Member organizations, a full-time staff, and the public work together to develop Web standards. W3C's mission is to lead the Web to its full potential by developing protocols and guidelines that ensure long-term growth for the Web.

History

W3C was founded in 1994 by Tim Berners-Lee, the inventor of the World Wide Web, and is now hosted by the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). It was created to lead the Web to its full potential by developing protocols and guidelines that ensure the Web's long-term growth.

Goals

The primary goals of W3C include ensuring that the Web remains accessible to everyone, improving Web interoperability, and developing new technologies to address emerging needs.
